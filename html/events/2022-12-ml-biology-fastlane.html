We present a simple method for improving large vision model performance on multiple downstream tasks. The current trend of research is to remove as much inductive bias as possible from model architectures and let them learn patterns from the data. We instead add inductive bias to the pre-training objective, rather than the model, in the form of domain-specific knowledge. Because we introduce inductive bias as a simple pre-training objective, the pre-trained weights are extremely flexible and can be easily applied to many tasks with no additional modification. We show that knowledge-guided pre-training improves pre-training convergence and downstream performance in many tasks and data distributions, especially in data-scarce regimes.
