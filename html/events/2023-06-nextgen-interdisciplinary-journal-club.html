Event details

Join us for this month's NextGen Interdiciplinary Journal Club, led by postdoc Chi Zhang, which will feature two articles related to using principal components from 3D morphometric data as traits. We will start with Gonzalez-Jose et al (2008) Cladistic analysis of continuous modularized traits provides phylogenetic signals in Homo evolution and move to Adams et al. (2011) Morphometrics and phylogenetics: Principal components of shape from cranial modules are neither appropriate nor effective cladistic characters, which is a response to Gonzalez-Jose et al.
The event will begin with a brief summary of each paper by Chi, followed by an open-ended group discussion around what variables generated from morphometrics should be used as traits. Please come prepared with your feedback, critiques and questions about the papers and methods used a considerations of this research within imageomics.
