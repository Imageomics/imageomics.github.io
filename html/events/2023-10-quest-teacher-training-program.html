Event details

At this month's Imageomics Research Forum, Dan Rubenstein and Leanna House will provide a recap of this past summer's QUEST Teacher Preparation Program. This summer the program featured In Our Back Yards: Human Impacts on Pollinator and Plant Populations in Local Environments. During QUEST, local educators posed questions of their own making about the ways humans impact pollinators in local environments. They were able to explore varied human impacts that range from taming wild lands to domesticated properties to the styles of farming that produce our food (organic vs. conventional). In this mini-research experience, educators ventured outdoors to take pictures of plants and the insects that use them. They then used images that characterize wild and domesticated habitats in terms of their diversity, distribution, size and connectivity in order to test hypotheses they generated about how such landscape features and other human impacts affect pollinators and the plants they rely on for survival and reproduction. By collaborating with peer educators, teachers model the interactions that their students will use when engaging in their own experiential learning activities during the school year. QUEST encourages educators to let their sense of wonder lead as they explore the process of scientific inquiry through images.
