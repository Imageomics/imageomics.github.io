Please join us Monday, April 20 @4pm EST on Zoom for the next Imageomics Seminar, Pixels are data: image analysis tools for known and unknown unknowns, with Dr. Beth Cimini.

Abstract: In the era of the digital camera, pixels from microscopy images aren't just about what your eyes can see anymore- correctly captured and analyzed, they're an incredibly rich data source. In this talk, we'll discuss approaches to use this data to address both the things you know you want to find in your sample, and previously-unsuspected connections between samples. We'll also discuss tools and resources for making it easier and faster to analyze whatever samples you have.

Speaker Bio: Dr. Beth Cimini is the Director of Bioimage Analysis at the Imaging Platform at the Broad Institute in Cambridge, MA.  She obtained a PhD in Biochemistry and Molecular Biology with Dr. Elizabeth Blackburn at UCSF followed by postdoctoral and staff scientist roles with Dr. Anne Carpenter's lab at the Broad, leading a team collaborating with ~30 outside scientists per year on custom image analysis projects.  The Cimini lab (est 2021) focuses on bioimage analysis tool creation (Piximi, Bilayers) and maintenance (CellProfiler), as well as on applying open source tools to novel biological problems.  She created and directs the Platform's Postdoctoral Training Program in Bioimage Analysis, and also leads the Broad efforts on community engagement and technology development for the Center for Open Bioimage Analysis (COBA).  She is a proud recipient of both the Association of Biomolecular Resource Facilities Alan Smith "Mentor of the Year" award as well as BioImaging North America's Michael W Davidson award for contributions in "Inclusion, Education, Mentorship, Standardization, and/or Advocacy".
