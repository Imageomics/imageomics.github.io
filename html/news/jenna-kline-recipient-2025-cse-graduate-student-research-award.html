April 7, 2025

Jenna Kline, Recipient of 2025 CSE Graduate Student Research Award

The Imageomics Institute is proud to announce Jenna Kline, a doctoral candidate in Computer Science and Engineering at The Ohio State University, is selected to receive the department’s prestigious 2025 Graduate Student Research Award.

Kline was recognized for her work integrating artificial intelligence, machine learning, and drone technology to enhance in situ wildlife monitoring. Her research develops autonomous aerial systems to optimize flight paths for capturing high-quality images of animals in their natural habitats—a vital step toward understanding species traits and behaviors at scale.

As a core contributor to the mission of the Imageomics Institute, Kline’s work helps advance the use of biological images to extract meaningful, machine-readable phenotypic information. Her efforts support the broader goal of building new data-driven tools bridging the gap between computer science and biology to accelerate discoveries in biodiversity and conservation science.

This award reflects Kline’s leadership in interdisciplinary research and her commitment to shaping the future of AI-powered ecological monitoring.
