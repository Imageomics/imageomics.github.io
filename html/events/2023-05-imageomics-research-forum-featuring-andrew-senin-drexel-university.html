This month’s research forum will feature the work of Andrew Senin of Drexel University.

Andrew Senin, a computer science major at Drexel University has been working under Dr. David Breen's and Dr. Jane Greenburg's guidance, with input from Tulane colleagues on automatic fish outlining and analysis as part of his senior design research project. Please join us as Andrew shares his research on Fourier descriptors as shape features for object (e.g. fish specimen) outlines.

Abstract: Outlines are a fundamental object of study in any morphometric analysis of biological specimens. They help answer scientific questions about a species' behavior, geography, and evolution. Online specimen image collections are a rich source of outlines, best used by extracting outlines via automated methods. But automated methods must account for significant variation in image quality and configuration, and represent extracted outlines in a way that facilitates answering the scientific question at hand. Our ongoing work extracts outlines from a subset of specimens in the INHS Fish Collection and explores what scientific questions they can answer with an elliptical Fourier analysis. Representing specimens' outlines by their elliptic Fourier descriptors, we've trained machine learning models to infer a specimen's species from its outline and identify distinguishing features of each species' outlines. Elliptic Fourier analysis also enables the visualizations of a species' average shape as well as cross-shapes between two or more specimens.
