About the Event

The Imageomics Institute is hosting a workshop as part of the Imageomics Conference, dedicated to bridging the gap between advanced computational tools and biological discovery. Within the field of imageomics, researchers have built a powerful suite of open-source tools; now, our mission is to evolve these into usable, community-wide resources. By partnering directly with biologists across all career stages, we aim to pressure-test existing workflows, uncover novel applications, and refine our messaging to ensure these tools are as intuitive as they are innovative. 

Motivation

The first generation of Imageomics tools has proven successful in specialized applications, but we believe their true impact lies in their versatility. To realize this, we want to move beyond technical development and focus on usability and outreach. We are seeking the 'biological perspective' to help us translate complex toolsets into usable assets. By gathering community feedback, we aim to refine our communication and identify novel use cases that empower biologists—regardless of their computational background—to see their data in a new light.

Scope

This is a high-engagement, hands-on workshop designed for co-development. Rather than merely a traditional demonstration, this workshop functions as an active partnership where tool refinement and novel use cases emerge through real-time collaboration. Participants will explore a curated suite of Imageomics tools - ranging from taxonomic identification to trait segmentation and classification. We will specifically examine how these technologies can be used by domain scientists, for instance, to measure certain functional traits (i.e., morphological and behavioral) in ecology, as well as extract complex behavioral data from video. 

Who Should Participate

We encourage participation from across the broader Imageomics Institute network, bringing together our biological and computational cohorts. This is a dedicated space for our ecologists and biologists, regardless of their technical expertise, to engage in a "deep dive" with our developers and data scientists. This internal partnership is vital to our iterative mission: leveraging our community’s diverse biological expertise to refine the Institute’s software tools and expand their research applications.

Goals & Desired Outcomes

This workshop seeks to:

  • Empower biologists and ecologists with technical proficiency in the Imageomics toolkit.
  • Co-develop novel workflows through real-time, iterative exercise of tool features.
  • Identify high-impact research applications and unmet use cases for image-based data.
  • Refine the Imageomics tool suite and value proposition to ensure clear, accessible communication for the broader life sciences community.
