Please join us on Monday, March 9th @4pm EST for the next Imageomics Seminar, From Images to Insight: Cloud-Based Morphometrics and AI Classification of 3D Knockout Embryos with Murat Maga! Dr. Maga is a Professor in the Division of Craniofacial Medicine in the Department of Pediatrics at the University of Washington and a member of our Imageomics community.

Abstract: Approximately 30% of genes are essential for mammalian development, yet we lack systematic tools to understand what happens when they malfunction. The International Mouse Phenotyping Consortium has generated thousands of high-resolution 3D scans of mouse embryos with specific gene knockouts—each representing a potential model for human birth defects. However, these extraordinary resources remain largely untapped because the severe malformations characteristic of these embryos defeat conventional image analysis methods, and modern artificial intelligence tools remain inaccessible to most biomedical researchers due to the complexity of managing the compute environments and workflows. I am going to present some of the tools we have been developing on how to tackle this challenge and make this data accessible and relevant for developmental genetics research.

Speaker Bio: Dr. Murat Maga is a quantitative morphologist broadly interested in genetics and morphometrics of development in mammals. He integrates high-resolution 3D imaging and computational analysis to investigate complex form-and-function relationships. His foundational research deciphered the genetic architecture of the mouse craniofacial system and drove the development of automated, reproducible 3D landmarking pipelines utilizing diffeomorphic image registration and synthetic atlases. Currently, as a Principal Investigator at the Seattle Children’s Research Institute (SCRI), he studies the genetic basis of normal phenotypic variability and the factors that perturb that.
