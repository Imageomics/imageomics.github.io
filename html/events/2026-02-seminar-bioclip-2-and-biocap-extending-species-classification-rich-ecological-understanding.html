Join us for our next seminar on February 9th at 4pm EST where Imageomics Postdoctoral Researcher, Dr. Jianyang Gu will discuss BioCLIP2 and BioCAP and how these tools can advance ecological research.

Abstract: Foundation models trained at scale can move beyond narrow recognition tasks and begin to encode higher-level structure about the world. In this talk, I will introduce BioCLIP 2, a large-scale vision-language model trained on 214M organismal images. The scaling leads to strong species classification performance, and more importantly, enables emergent ecological representations. Across benchmarks and case studies, I will highlight how the model supports practical workflows such as habitat classification and agricultural disease detection. I will then present BioCAP, a caption-augmented extension that enriches BioCLIP-style training with natural-language descriptions, improving the model’s ability to connect visual evidence with attributes, context, and broader ecological concepts. I will conclude with open challenges and opportunities on top of ecologically meaningful BioCLIP embeddings for the Imageomics community.

Speaker Bio: Jianyang Gu is a Postdoctoral Researcher at The Ohio State University. He works on machine learning, computer vision, multimodal understanding, and their applications in scientific discovery. He is particularly interested in enriching or condensing data to understand the contribution of each data point to machine learning models. He has co-organized the Dataset Distillation workshop in CVPR 2024, the Anomaly Detection in Scientific Domains Workshop, and the Imageomics Workshop in AAAI 2025 and NeurIPS 2025. He served as an area chair for ICLR 2026 and ICML 2026, and a reviewer for multiple journals and conferences, including IEEE T-PAMI, NeurIPS, CVPR, ICCV, etc.
