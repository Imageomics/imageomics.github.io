Imageomics Seminar: Dr. Maks Ovsjanikov, November 22, 2024

November 22, 2024, 10am ET

Title: Novel 3D deep learning approaches for robust shape comparison and analysis

Abstract: Recent years have witnessed several major advances in 3D data acquisition. However, techniques for processing and analyzing 3D data often lag behind their 2D counterparts. This is, in part, due to the challenges related to the lack of a canonical grid-like representation for 3D point clouds or surfaces, and, at the same time, the limited scope of data for training. In this talk, I will first describe a robust learning approach on curved surfaces using learned diffusion. I will then give several applications, including protein and RNA molecule analysis, cell tracking in 3D, and unsupervised representation learning via robust non-rigid 3D correspondence methods.

Bio: Maks Ovsjanikov is a Professor at Ecole Polytechnique in France. He works on 3D shape analysis with emphasis on deep learning techniques for shape matching and correspondence. He has received a Eurographics Young Researcher Award, an ERC Starting Grant, a CNRS Bronze Medal (highest recognition for junior researchers in France), and an ERC Consolidator Grant in 2023. His works have received 11 best paper awards or nominations at top conferences, including CVPR, ICCV, 3DV, as well as a SIGGRAPH 2023 Test-of-time award. His main research topics include 3D shape comparison and deep learning on 3D data.
