Marta Jarzyna, Assistant Professor, Evolution, Ecology and Organismal Biology, The Ohio State

Trait-based ecology has recently been heralded as the key to pinpointing relevant mechanisms and thus making generalizable ecological predictions. To date, however, lack of knowledge regarding the responses of specific organismal traits to environmental changes across spatiotemporal scales has hindered efforts to make trait-based ecology a predictive science. In my talk, I will discuss how expansive and growing repositories of species traits and their geographic distributions, often replicated across time, can help shed light on mechanisms underlying biodiversity dynamics across varied spatial and temporal scales.

Bio: Marta Jarzyna is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Evolution, Ecology and Organismal Biology at The Ohio State University. She is a broad-scale ecologist and global change biologist interested in understanding the processes underlying biodiversity dynamics across different spatial, temporal, and taxonomic scales.
