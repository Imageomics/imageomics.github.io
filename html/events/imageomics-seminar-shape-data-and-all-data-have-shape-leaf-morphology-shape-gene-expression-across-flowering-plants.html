Abstract

Embedded in plant morphology is genetic, developmental, and environmental information. Quantifying the plant form and disentangling the effects of these factors potentially allows us to breed better crops, influence growth and yield, and anticipate how plants will respond to a changing climate. I will begin with an introduction to geometric morphometrics and its application to model the shapes of leaves, as a simple example of a closed contour. I will then talk about X-ray Computed Tomography (CT) reconstructions of plants, which although capture exquisite details down to micron resolution, present problems in how information is extracted from this type of data. Consequently, we have been using Topological Data Analysis (TDA) in collaboration with the lab of Dr. Elizabeth Munch, the motto of which is “shape is data, and all data have shape”. I will provide a qualitative introduction to TDA and its application to quantify diverse plant morphologies. I will also present participatory student research using TDA to visualize the structure of more abstract data types, including the structure of gene expression profiles across the flowering plants.

Biography

Dan Chitwood grew up in the Central Valley of California, where he worked for Ernest & Julio Gallo Winery and was introduced to viticulture and the study of leaf shape. He earned his undergraduate degree in Genetics from University of California, Davis and his PhD from Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory on Long Island, where he studied the morphogenetic contribution of small RNAs to leaf patterning. Afterwards he returned to U.C. Davis where he studied the quantitative genetic basis of leaf shape in tomato and wild relatives. His lab at Michigan State University continues to study shape but more abstractly, applying TDA approaches to complex datasets, including X-ray CT reconstructions, 2D images, and large, publicly available molecular datasets. He creates interdisciplinary, intercultural, and bilingual curricula that involve students in participatory research across the US and México.
