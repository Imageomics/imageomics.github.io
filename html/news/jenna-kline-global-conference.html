Jenna Kline Participates in Global Conference

Jenna Kline represented the Imageomics Institute at the Global Conservation Tech and Drone Forum, speaking on how AI and data can scale species protection.

In her talk, she highlighted how tools like autonomous drones, computer vision, and open data are transforming biodiversity research which helps turn large volumes of ecological data into actionable insights. She also emphasized the importance of addressing global data gaps and supporting conservation efforts in underrepresented regions.

By showcasing projects like WildWing and the KABR dataset, Jenna demonstrated how imageomics is advancing real-time wildlife monitoring and shaping the future of AI-driven conservation.
