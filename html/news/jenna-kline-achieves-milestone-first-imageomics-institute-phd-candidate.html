November 26, 2024

Jenna Kline Achieves Milestone as First Imageomics Institute PhD Candidate

NextGen Jenna Kline officially passed her PhD candidacy exam, becoming the first doctoral candidate affiliated with the Imageomics Institute.

Kline's research at The Ohio State University exemplifies the institute’s mission to harness machine learning for biological discovery. Her work explores the use of autonomous unmanned aerial systems (UAS) for animal ecology, an innovative approach that combines engineering and artificial intelligence to study wildlife. By leveraging intelligent navigation models, edge infrastructure, and interdisciplinary collaboration, she aims to enable more precise and scalable ecological studies.

“The ability to deploy autonomous drones for real-time data collection in the field has transformative potential for animal ecology,” Kline said. “It’s exciting to see how technology can bridge the gap between computational advancements and pressing ecological questions.”

The Imageomics Institute, supported by the National Science Foundation, was established to develop tools that enable scientists to extract biological insights from image data. Kline’s research embodies this mission by applying machine learning techniques to generate novel ecological data, offering insights into species traits, behaviors, and their interactions with the environment.

Kline expressed gratitude to her advisors, including Drs. Tanya Berger-Wolf, Christopher Stewart, and DK Panda, among others, as well as the ICICLE AI Institute and Imageomics Institute for their support.

Her work aligns with Imageomics’ vision to create a collaborative research environment and inspire a new generation of scientists. Kline’s achievements not only advance ecological research but also demonstrate the power of interdisciplinary innovation.

“This is just the beginning,” Kline said. “I’m excited to continue exploring how AI-driven tools can expand our understanding of biodiversity and contribute to sustainable solutions for our planet.”

Kline’s journey highlights the convergence of cutting-edge technology and ecological discovery, setting a precedent for future research in the burgeoning field of Imageomics.
