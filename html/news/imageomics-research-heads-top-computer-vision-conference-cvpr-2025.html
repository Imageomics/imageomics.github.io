February 27, 2025

Imageomics Research Heads to Top Computer Vision Conference, CVPR 2025

Several Imageomics researchers will present their work at the prestigious IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR) 2025, happening June 11-15 at the Music City Center in Nashville, Tennessee. The conference is widely regarded as the leading annual event in computer vision, drawing top academics, industry experts, and students from around the world.

Among the accepted papers are three significant contributions from the Imageomics team, reflecting advances in fine-grained visual interpretation, explainable AI, and biological image analysis.

Advancing Visual Interpretability and AI Explainability

The paper "Prompt-CAM: A Simpler Interpretable Transformer for Fine-Grained Analysis" introduces a novel approach to improving how AI models interpret fine-grained visual concepts. Lead author Arpita Chowdhury, along with co-authors Dipanjyoti Paul, Zheda Mai, Jianyang Gu, Ziheng Zhang, Kazi Sajeed Mehrab, Elizabeth G. Campolongo, Daniel Rubenstein, Charles V. Stewart, Anuj Karpatne, Tanya Berger-Wolf, Yu Su, and Wei-Lun Chao, proposes a prompt-driven class attention map that enhances interpretability in AI-driven image recognition.

Another accepted paper, “Finer-CAM: Spotting the Difference Reveals Finer Details for Visual Explanation,” explores improving AI's ability to detect subtle differences in images, particularly for applications where detail is critical, such as species classification and medical imaging. Undergraduate researcher Ziheng Zhang is the lead author, working alongside Jianyang Gu, Arpita Chowdhury, Zheda Mai, David Carlyn, Tanya Berger-Wolf, Yu Su, and Wei-Lun Chao.

Biological Image Analysis for Conservation and Biodiversity

The team’s third paper, “Fish-Vista: A Multi-Purpose Dataset for Understanding & Identification of Traits from Images,” presents a new dataset designed to improve AI models in analyzing biological traits from images. This dataset has far-reaching implications for biodiversity studies, conservation efforts, and species identification. Lead author Kazi Sajeed Mehrab, along with co-authors M. Maruf, Arka Daw, Abhilash Neog, Harish Babu Manogaran, Mridul Khurana, Zhenyang Feng, Bahadir Altintas, Yasin Bakis, Elizabeth G. Campolongo, Matthew J. Thompson, Xiaojun Wang, Hilmar Lapp, Tanya Berger-Wolf, Paula Mabee, Henry Bart, Wei-Lun Chao, Wasila M. Dahdul, and Anuj Karpatne highlights the interdisciplinary collaboration between computer vision and the biological sciences.

Why Imageomics Research Matters to CVPR

CVPR, known for its high-caliber research and strong industry-academic connections, provides an ideal venue for Imageomics researchers to showcase how AI and computer vision can transform biological sciences. Imageomics focuses on using machine learning and visual analysis to decode patterns in biological data, aiding biodiversity monitoring, species classification, and conservation efforts. These contributions underscore the growing role of AI in solving pressing ecological and scientific challenges.
