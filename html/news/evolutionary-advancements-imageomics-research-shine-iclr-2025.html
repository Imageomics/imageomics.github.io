January 22, 2025

Evolutionary Advancements: Imageomics Research to Shine at ICLR 2025

Imageomics Institute research has been accepted to the 2025 International Conference on Learning Representations (ICLR), the world’s leading forum for advancements in deep learning and representation learning, drawing researchers, engineers, and innovators from across the globe to showcase transformative developments in AI. The ICLR conference will take place in Singapore, April 24 to 28. 

The accepted paper, titled "What Do You See in Common? Learning Hierarchical Prototypes over Tree-of-Life to Discover Evolutionary Traits," introduces a novel approach for uncovering evolutionary patterns using AI. This project combines hierarchical prototypes and explainable AI to analyze the Tree of Life, pushing the boundaries of computational biology and representation learning. The research builds on the Institute’s earlier work with PhyloNN, advancing explainable AI for trait discovery.

A second Imageomics-related paper unifying forward and inverse problems in subsurface imaging was also accepted, titled "A Unified Framework for Forward and Inverse Problems in Subsurface Imaging using Latent Space Translations."

The projects are a collaboration between Imageomics researchers and AI experts, led by Dr. Anuj Karpatne (Imageomics, Virginia Tech) and featuring contributions from Dr. Tanya Berger-Wolf (Imageomics, The Ohio State University), along with Harish Manogaran, Md Abdullah Al Maruf, and other key collaborators.

This accomplishment highlights the intersection of biodiversity and artificial intelligence, furthering Imageomics’ mission to drive biological insights through AI innovation. Congratulations to the team!
