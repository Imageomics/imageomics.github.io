Fishing for Data with Becky Fuller

Join us for a seminar hosted by the Imageomics Institute featuring Dr. Becky Fuller, Professor at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign and head of the Department of Evolution, Ecology, and Behavior.

  • What: Seminar – Fishing for Data: Three Vignettes on Image-Based Research in Fishes
  • When: Monday, April 14, 4 PM EST (Zoom)
  • Where: Register at go.osu.edu/becky-fuller

Explore how image-based methods are transforming our understanding of fish diversity and evolution. Dr. Fuller will present three research vignettes that illustrate the use of image data to study:

  1. The evolution of coloration in relation to lighting environments and visual systems

  2. The evolution of fin size and shape as it relates to sex and mating systems

  3. Speciation and the diagnosis of hybrids in nature

She will also discuss a new project using AI techniques to detect species and hybrids in collaboration with the Imageomics Group.

Bio:

Becky Fuller received her B.S. in Biology from the University of Nebraska. After a Fulbright fellowship abroad, she earned her M.S. from Michigan State University while stationed at the Kellogg Biological Station. She obtained her Ph.D. and completed a postdoc at Florida State University, working with Dr. Joe Travis. Since 2005, she has been a faculty member at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where she is now a full professor and head of the Department of Evolution, Ecology, and Behavior.
