Please join us on Monday, March 23rd at 4pm ET for an Imageomics Seminar, AI-based arthropod biodiversity on BugBox: Lessons learned over the first four years, with Kelton D. Welch, Research Entomologist and Collection Manager with the Ecdysis Foundation.

Abstract: BugBox is a web-based platform designed by Ecdysis Foundation to apply computer vision technology to arthropod biodiversity experiments. Over the last four years, Ecdysis Foundation has been using BugBox for rapid arthropod biodiversity assessments as part of our 1000 Farms Initiative, a broad-spectrum survey of regenerative agriculture systems in the USA and Canada. Here, I will review the learning process my team went through to build and deploy our BugBox platform, train our models and transition to a high-throughput workflow.

Speaker Bio: Kelton Welch is an entomologist who has studied ecology, spider behavior, biological control and general biodiversity. He was awarded a Ph.D. in Entomology from the University of Kentucky in 2013, and he has been working as a Research Entomologist and Collection Manager for Ecdysis Foundation since 2019, identifying hundreds of thousands of arthropods and building up the Mark F. Longfellow Ecological Reference Collection.
