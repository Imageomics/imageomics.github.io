Imageomics at NeurIPS: From Pixels to Principles

The Imageomics Institute will host the Imageomics Workshop at NeurIPS 2025 on Dec. 6 in San Diego, aiming to connect cutting-edge machine learning with urgent biodiversity and conservation needs.

The workshop, held during NeurIPS week, brings together computer scientists, ecologists, conservation practitioners and data stewards to tackle problems that arise when AI leaves the lab and meets the field. Organizers say the goal is to turn rapidly growing image, audio and sensor datasets—ranging from camera traps and drones to bioacoustics, museum collections and satellites—into interpretable, reproducible and actionable science.

Program themes include trait discovery and interpretability (e.g., learning biologically meaningful features from images and video), fine-grained recognition and population monitoring at scale, multimodal fusion across vision, acoustics, text and remote sensing, knowledge-guided ML using phylogenies and ontologies, data-centric and FAIR/AI-ready resources with transparent evaluation, and deployment on edge devices for real-world decision support.

The one-day event is expected to feature invited talks, peer-reviewed oral and poster presentations, and a panel focused on translating models into conservation outcomes. Submissions emphasizing reproducibility (code/data where possible), interpretability (mapping results to biology) and generalization (across sites, seasons and taxa) are encouraged.

The workshop aligns with the broader AI for Nature mission: building models that recover biological traits, scale across ecosystems, respect data ethics and produce outputs communities can use. By convening at NeurIPS, one of the world’s premier machine-learning venues, the workshop seeks to accelerate common benchmarks, shared tooling and collaborations between AI researchers and domain scientists.
