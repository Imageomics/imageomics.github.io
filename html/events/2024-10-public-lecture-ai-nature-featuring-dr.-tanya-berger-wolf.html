Event details

Please register to attend a Public Lecture hosted by the AI and Biodiversity Change Global Climate Center and the Montreal Botanical Gardens on Monday, October 7 at 4 PM ET (UTC-05:00), featuring speaker Dr. Tanya Berger-Wolf, Director of the Global Climate Center for AI and Biodiversity Change (ABC), a multi-national center funded by the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) and United States National Science Foundation (NSF).

The event will be live-streamed via zoom. Please register to receive the link.

Abstract

Dr. Berger-Wolf explores how AI is transforming the study and our decisions about nature by turning vast new data sources, such as GPS, high-definition cameras, autonomous vehicles under water, on the ground, and in the air, genotyping, acoustic sensors, and crowdsourcing, into high resolution information source about living organisms, enabling scientific inquiry, conservation, and policy decisions. She will present a vision and examples of AI as a trustworthy partner both in science and biodiversity conservation, discussing opportunities and challenges.

About ABC

The NSF and NSERC-funded AI and Biodiversity Change (ABC) Global Climate Center brings together scientists from around the world to develop new AI-enabled, data-supported approaches for understanding and responding to the impacts of climate change on biodiversity.
