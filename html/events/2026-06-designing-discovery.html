About the Event:

Imageomics Institute is hosting a half-day workshop at the Evolution Conference in Cleveland, Ohio dedicated to bridging the gap between advanced computational tools and biological discovery. Within the emerging field of imageomics, researchers have developed a growing suite of machine learning and computer vision tools designed to extract meaningful biological insight from images and videos. This workshop focuses on making these approaches more accessible, usable, and collaborative for the broader biology community.

Rather than serving solely as a technical demonstration, the workshop is designed as an interactive space where computational researchers and biologists can work together to explore workflows, discuss research challenges, and identify new scientific applications. Through tutorials, discussions, and collaborative exploration, participants will gain exposure to how imageomics methods can support biodiversity science, evolutionary biology, systematics, ecology, and behavioral analysis.

Who Should Participate:

We welcome participation from researchers, students, and professionals interested in the intersection of biology, evolution, ecology, and emerging computational methods. This workshop is designed to bring together biological and computational perspectives in a collaborative environment focused on discovery, experimentation, and co-development.

We especially encourage participation from:

  • Biologists and ecologists
  • Evolutionary biologists and systematists
  • Biodiversity researchers
  • Graduate students and early-career scientists
  • Computational researchers interested in biological applications
  • Educators and interdisciplinary collaborators
  • Anyone interested in AI, computer vision, and image-based biological research

This workshop is intended to be accessible to participants across all levels of computational experience. No advanced programming or machine learning background is required.

This workshop seeks to:

  • Empower biologists and ecologists with technical proficiency in the Imageomics toolkit.
  • Co-develop novel workflows through real-time, iterative exercise of tool features.
  • Identify high-impact research applications and unmet use cases for image-based data.
  • Refine the Imageomics tool suite and value proposition to ensure clear, accessible communication for the broader life sciences community.
