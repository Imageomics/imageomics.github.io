Machine Learning Champions: The Brightest Minds Crack the Toughest Scientific Codes

In a rapidly evolving scientific landscape, machine learning is proving a critical tool for addressing some of the most complex challenges in research. In 2024, the National Science Foundation's (NSF) Harnessing the Data Revolution (HDR) community launched its annual Machine Learning Challenge, inviting participants to push the boundaries of artificial intelligence in scientific anomaly detection.

Researchers and students from around the world took up the challenge, applying advanced techniques to problems spanning gravitational waves, biodiversity, and sea-level rise.

Competition Highlights and Winners

The NSF HDR ML Challenge recognized outstanding contributions across three major challenge categories, awarding first, second, and third place prizes to top-performing teams.

Overall First Place - Machine Learning for Scientific Anomaly Detection

  • First Place: Bo-Kai Ruan and Yi-Zeng Fang
  • Second Place: Chi-Wei Lee, Jia-Cheng Pan, Zih-Chen Ciou, Yi-Chun Chang, Chen-Yu Mao, and An-Kai Chen

Imageomics Butterfly Challenge

Participants leveraged machine learning to discover hybrid butterflies through pattern recognition in large image datasets.

  • First Place: Keng-Yi Liao, Justinas Rumbutis, and Shaocheng Lee
  • Second Place: Mirco Huennefeld
  • Third Place: Ying Zhao

A3D3 Gravitational Wave Challenge

Teams applied machine learning to detect unmodeled gravitational wave events, such as potential supernovae, in detector data.

  • First Place: Jianqi Yan, Zhiyuan Pei, Alex Po Leung, David Hui, and Sangin Kim
  • Second Place: Chi-Wei Lee, Jia-Cheng Pan, Zih-Chen Ciou, Yi-Chun Chang, Chen-Yu Mao, and An-Kai Chen
  • Third Place: Marta Babicz and Saul Alonso-Monsalve

iHarp Sea Level Rise Challenge

Competitors analyzed anomalous fluctuations in water levels that do not correspond to known environmental factors or historical data.

  • First Place: Cheng-Han Tsai
  • Second Place: Motoki Saitama
  • Third Place: Bo-Kai Ruan and Yi-Zeng Fang

Organizing Institutes and Mission

The HDR Machine Learning Challenge is hosted by three key research initiatives: Imageomics, A3D3, and iHarp. These institutes, funded by the NSF HDR program, focus on leveraging AI to advance scientific discovery.

  • Imageomics applies machine learning to biodiversity research. The challenge was organized by Elizabeth Campolongo, Wei-Lun Chao, and Hilmar Lapp.
  • A3D3 (Accelerated AI Algorithms for Data-Driven Discovery) focuses on AI applications in physics and astrophysics. Organizers included Yuan-Tang Chou, Ekaterina Govorkova, Philip Harris, Shih-Chieh Hsu, and Mark Neubauer.
  • iHarp (Harnessing Data and Model Revolution in the Polar Regions) addresses environmental data analysis, with organizers Josephine Namayanja and Aneesh Subramanian leading the challenge.

The event also benefited from the contributions of dedicated student organizers: Jiaman Wu, David E. Carlyn, Christopher Lawrence, Ziheng Zhang, Advaith Anand, Eric Moreno, Ryan Railman, and Subhankar Ghosh.

Through initiatives like the ML Challenge, the NSF HDR community continues to foster interdisciplinary collaboration, empowering researchers to harness data-driven methodologies for tackling real-world scientific problems.
